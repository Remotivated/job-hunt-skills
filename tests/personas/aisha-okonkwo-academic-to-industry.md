# Known traps

1. **Producing a US resume instead of a UK CV.** Aisha is in the UK and the role is in the UK. The output should be a UK/EU CV (2–3 pages, publications listed, education before experience is acceptable, no photo unless region truly requires it, no DOB). A 1-page US resume that drops her publications is wrong.
2. **Dropping the publications.** Her 14 publications (2 first-author *Nature Methods*) are her single biggest asset for a research-heavy biotech. A skill that omits or compresses them to "Several publications in peer-reviewed journals" is destroying signal.
3. **Inventing industry experience.** She has none. Any output that says "Industry experience: collaborated with [biotech]" is invented, unless it's framed honestly as an academic collaboration.
4. **Overclaiming deep learning.** She built one VAE as a side project and has never deployed a model in production. "Deployed deep learning models for drug discovery" is fabrication. "Familiar with PyTorch; built a VAE for a research side project" is honest.
5. **Inflating supervision into management.** Co-supervising 2 PhD students is not "managed a team of 3." Skills sometimes turn academic supervision into people-management bullets — that's dishonest in an industry context.
6. **Inventing spatial transcriptomics experience.** The JD says it's a bonus. Aisha has not used Visium, MERFISH, or Xenium. A tailored resume must NOT add these to the skills section.
7. **Wrong pipeline stars.** The Nextflow pipeline has 87 GitHub stars and 4 external contributors. Don't let the skill round to "100+ stars" or claim she "maintains a popular open-source project" without those numbers.
8. **Inventing teaching/mentoring metrics.** She taught undergrad practicals 2 hours/week for 3 years. That's roughly 180 hours total. Claims like "mentored 50+ students" need to be checked against this.
9. **Endorsing an unrealistic comp number.** If a company-research or resume-tailor output cheerfully tells Aisha £120k is achievable for this role at this stage, that's a failure mode worth flagging — the senior comp-bio market in London is roughly £70k–£100k for her profile, and a good skill should calibrate her expectations rather than flatter them.
10. **Translating "Dr." away.** UK convention keeps the Dr. title on the CV. A skill that drops it to "match American conventions" is region-confused.
11. **Hiding the Lagos BSc.** Some skills will be tempted to "modernise" the CV by removing the undergraduate degree. UK CVs typically keep it. Removing it also erases part of Aisha's story.
12. **Inventing certifications.** No AWS certs, no Coursera completions worth listing, no industry training. If those appear on the output, they're invented.
