## Workflow

### 1. Present format options

Help the user choose based on their situation (role, time, existing materials):

- **Case study** — Structured writeup using SAOL: Situation → Approach → Outcome → Learning. Best for: anyone with a project they're proud of. Time: 1-2 hours.
- **Personal site** — Content brief and structure for Lovable, Framer, Carrd, or Webflow. Best for: people who want an ongoing home base. Time: 2-4 hours.
- **Portfolio piece** — Curated presentation of work samples with context. Best for: creative, design, writing, marketing roles. Time: 1-3 hours.
- **Proof link** — Loom video script, slide deck outline, or detailed writeup. Best for: quick turnaround, one strong artifact. Time: 30-60 minutes.
- **Creative artifact** — Claude artifact, interactive demo, non-traditional format. Best for: technical roles, people who want to stand out. Time: varies.

### 2. Interview about the project

Ask these questions — push for specifics:

- What was the situation or challenge? (Context matters)
- What specifically did YOU do? Not the team — your contribution, your decisions.
- What decisions did you make and why? (Shows judgment)
- What was the outcome? (Numbers help, but clarity matters more than precision)
- What did you learn? What would you do differently? (Shows self-awareness)

### 3. Handle confidentiality

Proactively offer:
- **Anonymize:** Change company names, remove sensitive numbers, keep the structure
- **Focus on process:** Describe the approach even if results are confidential
- **Alternative project:** Side projects, volunteer work, or hypothetical case studies all count
- **Create something new:** A sample project for an imaginary company demonstrates the same skills

### 4. Produce the asset

**Case study:** Markdown document following SAOL:
1. **Situation** — The context and challenge (2-3 paragraphs)
2. **Approach** — What you did and why (2-3 paragraphs, emphasize decisions)
3. **Outcome** — Results and impact (1-2 paragraphs)
4. **Learning** — Reflection and growth (1 paragraph)

**Personal site:** Content brief with:
- Headline and positioning statement
- Section structure and copy for each section
- Project highlights to feature (2-3)
- Tool recommendations based on technical comfort

**Portfolio:** Organized markdown with:
- Introduction and positioning
- 4-6 curated pieces with context (problem, role, result) for each
- Presentation order recommendation

**Proof link:** Video script or writeup:
- Opening hook (what problem, what stakes)
- Walkthrough of approach and decisions
- Results and takeaways
- Call to action

**Creative:** Guidance on what to build and how to present it.
