# NorthwindSync demo-environment refactor — case study

**Chosen idea:** A written case study of the Docker + Python demo stack I designed at NorthwindSync that cut SE demo prep time from ~4 hours to ~45 minutes and became the team standard.

**Why it fits:** Mid-market data-tooling SE roles (Polaris Data, similar postings) explicitly want evidence of *technical depth applied to GTM* — not just "I can demo," but "I built the infrastructure my team demos on." This is the strongest single piece of evidence I have for that exact bar. Closes the "show me the technical work, not just outcomes" gap that every Senior SE posting asks for.

**Rough scope:**

- ~1,500-word write-up structured as Problem → Approach → Implementation → Adoption → Reflection.
- Anonymize the employer (NorthwindSync → "a B2B data-integration SaaS") and the specific prospect profiles.
- Include 2-3 architecture sketches (mermaid or hand-drawn): the old per-call build vs. the standardized stack, plus the prospect-profile branching logic.
- Do NOT include real source code. The point is the design decisions and adoption mechanics, not a working repo.
- Effort: ~6 hours drafting + ~2 hours diagrams. Single weekend.

**What it proves:**

- Can build internal tooling that ships, not just slide decks.
- Understands SE team economics (prep time as the constrained resource).
- Knows how to drive adoption without formal authority — the reflection beat in the story-bank entry is the seniority signal here.
