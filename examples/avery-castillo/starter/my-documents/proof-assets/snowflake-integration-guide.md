# Snowflake integration guide — proof link

**Chosen idea:** A linkable public artifact pointing to the Snowflake permission-grants integration guide I authored at NorthwindSync, paired with a one-paragraph context note explaining what shipped and why it mattered.

**Why it fits:** Data-tooling SE postings consistently ask for evidence the candidate can *write to a customer audience*, not just talk in a demo. The guide is already public, already cited by support, and already shipped — the "proof asset" work here is mostly framing, not production. Closes the "can this SE communicate in writing under their own name" gap that interviewers can't probe in a 30-minute screen.

**Rough scope:**

- Short framing post (~400 words) on personal site or LinkedIn long-form: what the guide solved (recurring permission-misconfiguration tickets), how I scoped it (last 30 support tickets), what changed after publication (6 tickets/week → ~1/week within two months).
- Direct link to the published guide on NorthwindSync's docs hub.
- No anonymization needed — the guide is already public under my byline.
- Effort: ~2 hours. This is the lowest-effort proof asset I have available because the artifact already exists.

**What it proves:**

- Customer-facing technical writing that survives real-world use.
- Cross-functional collaboration with support and docs (story-bank entry `integration-guide-snowflake` has the receipts).
- Measurable downstream impact on support load — concrete, defensible, not inflated.

**Acceptance criteria:**

- [ ] Framing post leads with the *problem* the guide solved, not "I wrote a guide."
- [ ] Measurable outcome (6/wk → 1/wk) is stated with the caveat that support tooling and product changes also moved during that window.
- [ ] Link to the live guide is the first inline link in the post.
- [ ] Pinned on LinkedIn Featured.
