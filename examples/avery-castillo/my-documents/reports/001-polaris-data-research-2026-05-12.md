# Polaris Data — Research Report

## At-a-glance

- **Stage:** Series B (raised $42M in 2024)
- **Headcount:** ~80, GTM ~25
- **Product:** Real-time data ingestion / reverse-ETL hybrid targeting mid-market analytics teams
- **Why interesting for Avery:** Direct overlap with NorthwindSync's segment and competitive set; product is materially different in the ingestion model.

## Product depth

The Polaris Data product wedges between traditional ETL (Fivetran, Airbyte) and reverse-ETL (Hightouch, Census) by ingesting and immediately routing data based on customer-defined business events. The technical claim is that this collapses two pipelines into one for the typical mid-market analytics stack. Two design-partner case studies on the public site document 4–8× pipeline reduction and ~40% lower warehouse compute cost.

Competitive read: this is a genuine product differentiator, not just a positioning play. If the customer-cited compute savings hold across more accounts, the product has real pull.

## GTM signals

- Two co-founders (technical + GTM) both publicly write about an "SE-led discovery" sales motion — the SE org has explicit authority in qualification, not just demos.
- 6 SE openings posted in last 60 days (per LinkedIn) — meaningful org growth.
- Glassdoor sample size is small (~20 reviews) but trends positive on engineering culture; one mentioned concern around comp-band tightness for senior ICs.

## Risks

- Series B, two-year runway visible from $42M raise and ~80 headcount but no public ARR. Reasonable for stage but not bulletproof.
- Customer base reads heavily mid-market — if enterprise upmarket is the 2026 thesis, expect the SE motion to shift significantly within the year.
- Comp-band tightness flag warrants a direct question during interviews.

## Recommended interview talking points
