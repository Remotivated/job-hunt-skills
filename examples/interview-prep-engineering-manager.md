# Interview Prep - Engineering Manager, Payments Platform | Modal Labs

**Candidate:** Maya Chen  
**Role:** Engineering Manager, Payments Platform  
**Company:** Modal Labs (Series B, ~60-person eng org, remote US)  
**Date:** 2026-04-11

## Angle to Lead With

You are a backend tech lead with 9 years of experience who is already operating as an EM - running 1:1s for three ICs, owning the team's hiring rubric and 31 interview loops in 2024, designing the on-call rotation, and mentoring two engineers through promotion to mid-level - without the formal title because Lattice Pay's management-track promos are frozen. The Modal JD asks for "2+ years of EM experience or strong tech lead experience with formal people-management responsibilities," which is the exact bridge you need.

## Likely Questions

1. **Walk me through your background and why you're looking for an EM role.**
   - 9 years backend -> Stripe (Radar) -> tech lead at Lattice Pay since April 2024.
   - Honest framing: the title is frozen, not the work.

2. **Tell me about a major technical migration you've led.**
   - Story: Rails-to-Go disbursement migration ($140M/month, 7 months, p99 1,800ms -> 240ms).

3. **How do you design and run an on-call rotation?**
   - Story: On-call paging reduction (22 -> 6 pages/week across 2023).

4. **Tell me about a time you helped an engineer grow into the next level.**
   - Story: mentoring two engineers through promotion to mid-level in 2024.

5. **Walk me through your hiring philosophy. How would you grow this team from 5 to 8?**
   - Story: authored the backend hiring rubric, sat on 31 loops in 2024, hired two of the team's current four members.

6. **Tell me about a time you had a difficult performance conversation.**
   - Honest gap: no PIP experience.
   - Bridge: name the closest real experience - weekly 1:1 gap conversations and promotion packet work - and be direct that this is a first-time-manager growth area.

7. **How do you balance shipping velocity with reliability for payment-critical services?**
   - Combine the migration story with the on-call paging-reduction story.

8. **Tell me about a time you disagreed with a peer or your manager.**
   - If you do not have a clean story ready, pause and give a real answer rather than manufacture one.

## Questions to Ask Modal

**How they work**

- How does the Payments Platform EM split time between people work and technical work?
- What does the on-call rotation look like today, and what is the EM's role in it?

**Career growth**

- What does the growth path from EM look like at Modal?
- How does Modal support a first-time EM specifically?

**Culture**

- How does the team handle disagreements between an EM and a senior IC on technical direction?
- Walk me through a recent incident and what changed because of it.

**Remote operations**

- What is the time-zone distribution of the team today, and what does sync vs async default look like?
- What does the hiring loop look like for backend ICs, and would I own it from day one?

## Potential Weaknesses

| Gap | Honest acknowledgment | Bridge |
| --- | --------------------- | ------ |
| No formal EM title | "My title is Tech Lead, not Engineering Manager." | "The work is the same: 1:1s, hiring loops, promotions, and team-process ownership." |
| No PIP / termination experience | "I have never run a PIP or been part of letting someone go." | "I would be developing that muscle in this role for the first time, and I would want to understand what support looks like." |
| Not previously fully remote | "Lattice Pay is hybrid, not fully remote." | Surface async behaviors: documentation, runbook design, and written 1:1 notes. |
| No deep fintech compliance background | "My fintech work has been on the engineering reliability side, not the regulatory/compliance side." | Be direct about the boundary and do not pretend otherwise. |
