# Interview Prep

## When to Use

When you have an upcoming interview and want to prepare with likely questions, talking points drawn from your actual experience, and smart questions to ask them.

## What You'll Need

- The job posting (paste the full text)
- Company name
- Your resume (paste the full text)
- Anything you know about the company (optional — culture, recent news, how they work)

## The Prompt

```
Help me prepare for an interview. I want to evaluate THEM as much as they evaluate ME.

COMPANY: [company name]

JOB POSTING:
[paste the job posting here]

MY RESUME:
[paste your resume here]

WHAT I KNOW ABOUT THE COMPANY (optional):
[paste any info — their website, culture page, recent news, Glassdoor notes, or "nothing yet"]

GENERATE:

1. **LIKELY QUESTIONS (8-12)**
   Generate questions I'm likely to face based on the role requirements:
   - 3-4 behavioral questions specific to this role
   - 2-3 technical or domain questions from the posting's requirements
   - 2-3 remote-work questions ("How do you handle async communication?", "Describe working independently on a complex project")

   For EACH question: Provide talking points drawn from MY ACTUAL RESUME — reference specific achievements, not generic advice.

2. **QUESTIONS I SHOULD ASK (5-8)**
   Questions that show sophistication and help me evaluate the company. Categorize:

   **How They Work:**
   - "Walk me through a typical week. How much is meetings vs. focused work?"
   - "How do decisions get made and communicated?"

   **Career Growth:**
   - "How do people in this role grow and advance?"
   - "What does success look like in the first six months?"

   **Culture:**
   - "What's the hardest part about working here?"
   - "How does the team handle disagreements?"

   For each: Include green flags (good answers) and red flags (concerning answers) to watch for.

3. **ANGLES TO HIGHLIGHT**
   - My 3 strongest selling points for THIS role (with specific evidence)
   - Remote-readiness talking points
   - The narrative to lead with

4. **POTENTIAL WEAKNESSES**
   - Gaps between my resume and their requirements
   - How to address each honestly (acknowledge + redirect to strengths)
   - Don't spin or hide — self-awareness is a green flag
```

## What to Expect

A comprehensive interview prep brief with role-specific questions, talking points tied to your actual experience, evaluative questions to ask them (with signals to watch for), and honest assessment of your weaknesses for this role.
