## Personal Statement

<!-- UK/EU CVs typically open with a 2-4 sentence Personal Statement (also
     called a "Profile" or "Summary"). This is the "who I am and why I want
     this job" paragraph — more prominent and more common than the optional
     Summary on a US resume.

     Write it per-application if you can. First-person is fine ("I build...")
     but avoid the 1990s third-person convention ("Sarah is a results-driven
     professional...") — it reads as dated.

     Good: "Backend engineer with six years building high-throughput payment
     systems in Python and Go. Led the migration of a legacy monolith to
     microservices at [Company], reducing deploy time from two hours to eight
     minutes. Looking to bring the same ownership and async-first mindset to
     [Target Company]'s platform team."

     Bad: "Highly motivated and enthusiastic professional with a proven track
     record of delivering results in dynamic environments." (Says nothing.) -->
